THE TOP

The badminton world has witnessed the torch of greatness pass from generation to generation, with Yonex proudly witnessing legends emerge. Lee Chong Wei, the maestro of finesse and resilience, reigned supreme for a record 349 weeks as world No. 1, carving his name in history. Now, the mantle falls upon Viktor Axelsen, a powerhouse of athleticism and tactical brilliance, who currently holds the coveted top spot. Both, united by the Yonex legacy, etched their names in the sport's elite, each representing a distinct era of badminton mastery. While Lee's artistry enthralled audiences with deceptive drops and lightning-fast footwork, Axelsen's electrifying smashes and strategic adaptability captivate the modern scene. Their journeys, though separated by time, share a common thread: relentless pursuit of excellence, fueled by the Yonex spirit. Whether you hail the old guard's elegance or the new wave's dynamism, one thing remains clear: Yonex continues to nurture champions, shaping the ever-evolving landscape of badminton.
